Weighted Efficiency Score (WES)

WES measures both success and efficiency, penalizing slow agents even if they succeed, and rewarding fast failures over slow ones.

Successful Task: $r_t = 1$

$$ WES^{+} = \frac{t_{\text{human}}}{t_{\text{agent}}} $$

A higher score is awarded for using fewer agent steps ($t_{\text{agent}}$) relative to the expected human steps ($t_{\text{human}}$).

Failed Task: $r_t = 0$

$$ WES^{-} = - \left( \frac{t_{\text{agent}}}{S} \right) $$

A smaller penalty is assigned for failing in fewer agent steps ($t_{\text{agent}}$) relative to the maximum steps allowed ($S$).

The total score, aggregated across all tasks, ranges from -1 to 1, providing a comprehensive measure of an agent's combined accuracy and efficiency.
